How we started

From a Mumbai garage to processing ₹2 lakh crore annually

In 2018, co-founders Arjun Desai and Kavya Menon were building a small SaaS product for logistics companies. Getting paid was harder than building the software itself — weeks of onboarding, a minimum GMV requirement they couldn't yet meet, and an integration that broke every time a bank changed its API.

They stopped building the logistics product and started building the payment infrastructure they wished existed. FinPay launched in beta with 12 merchants. Within six months, 3,000 businesses had signed up without a single paid acquisition.

Today, FinPay powers payments for over a million businesses across India and 180 countries — from freelancers sending their first invoice to multinational enterprises running subscription businesses at scale. The frustration that started it all is still what drives every product decision we make.

Mission

Make payments a solved problem for every business

We believe that the complexity of accepting money online should be entirely invisible to businesses. FinPay's job is to absorb every layer of that complexity — banking relationships, payment method proliferation, compliance, fraud, currency conversion — and expose a single, clean interface that just works.

Vision

A world where any business can sell to anyone, anywhere

Geography, currency, and local regulation should never be the reason a business can't reach a customer. We're building the global payment layer that removes those barriers — not just for companies that can afford enterprise contracts, but for every business from day one.

Timeline

Seven years of building

The milestones that shaped FinPay from a two-person prototype to a global payments infrastructure company.

  1. 2018

    The beginning

    Arjun Desai and Kavya Menon incorporate FinPay in Mumbai. The first version supports only cards and net banking with a three-day integration time. Twelve merchants sign on.

  2. 2019

    Series A & first scale

    Raised ₹180 crore Series A. UPI integration launched, reducing integration time to 15 minutes. Monthly GMV crosses ₹100 crore for the first time.

  3. 2020

    Subscriptions & 100K milestone

    Launched the subscriptions product during the shift to remote work and SaaS. Reached 100,000 active merchants. Opened Bangalore engineering hub.

  4. 2021

    Going global

    International payments launched across 60 countries. Series C round at a $2B valuation. First overseas office opened in Singapore to support Southeast Asian expansion.

  5. 2022

    MENA expansion & 500K merchants

    Dubai office opened to serve the MENA corridor. Half a million merchants active on the platform. FinPay Payment Pages launched — no-code checkout for non-technical users.

  6. 2023

    Intelligence & reliability

    Smart Retry ML engine launched, recovering 18% of failed payments on average. Introduced our 99.99% uptime SLA. PCI DSS Level 1 re-certification completed.

  7. 2024

    One million merchants

    FinPay crosses the one million active merchant milestone and ₹2 lakh crore in annual GMV. Ranked #3 in India's most trusted fintech brands survey.

  8. 2025

    Enterprise & global infrastructure

    FinPay Enterprise launched with dedicated infrastructure and custom commercial terms. Team grows past 2,400 people across seven countries. Expansion into Latin America underway.

Core Values

How we make decisions

Not a list of aspirations — these are the principles we use to resolve disagreements and make hard calls.

◈

Merchant obsession

Every product decision starts with the merchant's experience. We measure success by how much complexity we've removed from the person trying to build a business, not by features shipped.
◈

Reliability as a promise, not a metric

When money is involved, downtime is not a performance issue — it's a trust issue. We hold ourselves to standards where 99.99% is the floor, not the goal, and we own every failure completely.
◈

Radical transparency

Our fees are published. Our incidents are public. Our API deprecation timelines are posted months in advance. Merchants and developers should never have to guess what FinPay is doing or why.
◈

Build for scale from day one

A payment system that works at 100 transactions per month must also work at 100,000 per second. We design with both in mind, refusing the shortcut of "we'll fix it when it becomes a problem."
◈

Inclusion drives better products

The businesses using FinPay span every Indian state, 180 countries, and dozens of languages. Our team needs to reflect that diversity of perspective — it makes our product more robust, not just more representative.
◈

Move fast without breaking merchants

Speed matters in a competitive market, but not at the cost of stability for the businesses that depend on us. We ship fast — but behind feature flags, with rollback plans, and always with merchant communication before any breaking change.
